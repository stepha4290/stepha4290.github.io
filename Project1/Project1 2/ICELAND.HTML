Iceland

Iceland is an island located midway between North America and mainland Europe. Iceland is known for being the Land of fire and ice. It is home to some of the world's most active volcanos and home to some of the largest glaciers in Europe.

Things to do in Iceland.


Northen Lights in Iceland

The Northern Lights also known as Aurora Borealis is one of the world's greatest natural spectacles.
In Norse mythology viewing this spectacle meant they were getting a glance of the Valkyries; the female figures of mythology who decided who lived and died in battle.
The natural light show begins when disturbances on the sun pull on Earth's magnetic field. This creates Alfvèn waves that sendoff electrons at high speeds into Earth's atmosphere where they create the aurora.




Ice climbing and hiking on glaciers

When visiting Iceland, one must explore the enormous glaciers.
One of the easiest glacial walks takes you to Sòlheimajökull, a beautiful outlet glacier from the Mýrdalsjökull ice cap.
Just under the cap lies the Katla caldeira which is one of Iceland's biggest volcano.
